Directory for Wildlife

Unseen. Unloved. Sometimes - even killed.

For most wildlife, these are something they seldomly face. But for the others, these are their daily truth - judged and stereotyped. Most people fear them, and others go as far as hurting them and killing them in the process. They say they do it out of fear, that these creatures are dangerous and that what they did is a form of self-protection. But the truth is far from that. In reality, these beings thought to be deadly are not at all harmful and most probably, are harmless. When these beings bite, the number one reason for it would be that it's being defensive. You might have unawarely harmed it, thus triggering its defense mechanism.

To prevent such accidents from happening, it is important for us people to learn their patterns and behaviors. With this website, we can help spread awareness on wildlife handling and unnecessary killing of these unseen beings.

Wildlife Classification:

Reptiles

Dumeril's Wolfsnake

Scientific Name: Lycodon dumerilii
Specification: Non-Venomous
Diet: primarily feeds on small lizards
Size: maximum length of about 0.75 meters
Pattern: Transverse light bands separated by wide dark interspaces
Typically found in: forested and ecotone habitats

Samar Cobra

Scientific Name: Naja samarensis
Specification: Highly Venomous
Diet: primarily eats small rodents
Size: maximum length of up to 1.4 meters
Pattern: coloration of black and yellow to green
Typically found in: mountainous jungle, tropical plains, and sometimes close to human settlements

Philippine Vine Snake

Scientific Name: Ahaetulla prasina preocularis
Specification: Mildly Venomous
Diet: primarily eats lizards and frogs
Size: maximum length of about 1.80 meters
Appearance: has a long snout with blunt end, with its coloration from brown to bright green, often with yellow line on lower flanks
Typically found in: areas with dense vegetation like trees, shrubs, and vines

Yellow-Headed Water Monitor Lizard

Scientific Name: Varanus cumingi
Specification: Mildly Venomous
Diet: primarily eats rodents, birds, and fishes
Size: can reach a length of up to 1.5 meters
Appearance: large lizard with the highest degree of yellow coloration
Typically found in: mangrove, forests, and water margins in tropical refuges

Philippine Sailfin Lizard

Scientific Name: Hydrosaurus pustulatus
Specification: Non-Venomous
Diet: primarily eats leaves, fruits, insects, and crustaceans
Size: can reach a length of up to 2-3 feet
Appearance: notable for its prominent sail-like dorsal crest extending from the base of its tail to the lower back
Typically found in: tropical wooded areas near various bodies of water

Green Crested Lizard

Scientific Name: Bronchocela cristatella
Specification: Non-Venomous
Diet: primarily eats leaves, fruits, insects, and crustaceans
Size: 57 cm
Appearance: medium-sized lizard displaying bright grass-green or jade-green coloration
Typically found in: found in forests as well as parks and rural areas

Philippine Box Turtle

Scientific Name: Cuora philippinensis
Diet: includes both plant and animal matter.
Appearance: small, roughly the size of a human palm
Typically found in: lowland freshwater habitats including ponds, marshes, rice paddies, irrigation canals, and drainage ditches

Mammals

Philippine tomb bat

Scientific Name: Taphozous melanopogon phillipinensis
Diet: insects
Appearance: may reach a length of 130mm, with a fur of white and tipped with pale brown to reddish-white
Typically found in: rainforests and woodlands.

Asian Palm Civet

Scientific Name: Paradoxurus hermaphroditus
Diet: fruits such as berries and pulpy fruits
Appearance: long, stocky body is covered with coarse, shaggy hair that is usually greyish in colour
Typically found in: forests

Philippine Tarsier

Scientific Name: Carlito syrichta
Diet: insects, spiders, small crustaceans, and small vertebrates
Appearance: 3.35 to 6.30 inches in height, with disproportionately large eyes
Typically found in: dense, low-level vegetation with perching sites

Amphibians and Invertebrates

Swamp Frog (Small Disked Frog)

Scientific Name: Limnonectes leytensis
Diet: invertebrates and smaller amphibians
Appearance: medium-sized frog with a depressed body shape and dorsolateral eye position
Typically found in: wet and moist places with water sources

Everett's Frog

Scientific Name: Sanguirana everetti
Diet: small invertebrates such as insects, spiders, and other arthropods commonly found in forest stream environments
Appearance: body is moist with green with brown stripe coloration
Typically found in: undisturbed and disturbed streams and rivers within lower montane and lowland forests

Pantropical Huntsman Spider

Scientific Name: Heteropoda venatoria
Diet: feeds on insects, which includes various species of butterflies and moths
Appearance: flat, brown body, with length spanning 9-12 cm
Typically found in: homes and buildings
